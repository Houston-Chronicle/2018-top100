Alison Cook introduces Houston's Top 100 restaurants

This year's list of the city's Top 100 restaurants is hardly a tablet handed down from the mount. It's a variegated mosaic of what matters to a single critic, one who has made it her business to think about Houston restaurants for over four decades.

Food is always the primary consideration in these rankings, as is the consistence of its delivery. If your favorite place has gone missing, it may well be that it disappointed upon a recent revisit. Or it may simply mean that there is heightened competition in its category this year.

Ambition matters, whether it is broad-based or tightly focused. What doesn't matter is whether the food is costly or affordable, or whether the surroundings are luxurious. A sense of Houston place, however, is one of those intangibles that can make a difference. Service and attention to beverages can burnish a ranking, too.

Then there is the curious lightning that can strike: the feeling of joy a restaurant, a room, a certain dish can evoke. It's hard to pin down, but all the restaurants on this list have given me that gift.

You may notice that there are 30 ranked restaurants instead of the top 25 that were ranked last year. So rich and exciting has been our restaurant scene of late that expanding the rankings seemed like a sensible way to acknowledge this year's talent pool.

As always, this list is designed to be the subject of argument and debate. A food scene without a healthy dialectic is not much of a food scene at all. The only sure thing? Next year will be a different story. And that's the fun of it.

-Alison Cook

About Alison Cook

Alison Cook has been the Houston Chronicle’s restaurant critic since 2002. She has won two James Beard Foundation awards for restaurant criticism, as well as the coveted M.F.K. Fisher Award for Distinguished Writing. Cook’s writing also has appeared in Texas Monthly, Esquire, GQ, The New York Times Magazine, Gourmet, Saveur, Food & Wine, Vogue, Travel & Leisure and House & Garden.
